The Zephyr Initiative

Published Jan. 2, 2148

By Sameer Dalal

Pulsar has announced plans to launch a new mission called the Zephyr Initiative to study exoplanets, including one of the youngest exoplanets ever discovered by astronomers.

The mission will involve the use of advanced telescopes and instruments to study exoplanets and their surrounding environments. Pulsar's goal is to research more exoplanets for habitation along with their space tourism program. By studying these exoplanets, Pulsar hopes to gain a better understanding of their potential for human habitation and what challenges must be overcome in order to make them suitable for long-term stays. Pulsar believes that this research will not only help advance the field of space exploration, but also contribute to the future of humanity as we look towards potential off-world settlements, providing unique opportunities for individuals to experience space travel while also supporting the company's research efforts. One of the key targets of the mission will be the Agenon-901 exoplanet located 390 light-years from Earth. This planet is just 3 million years old, making it one of the youngest exoplanets ever discovered. By studying this planet and its surrounding environment, scientists hope to gain insights into how planets form and evolve over time.

The mission is expected to launch in the next few years, and Pulsar is currently assembling a team of experts in astronomy, astrophysics, and planetary science to lead the research. The company is also seeking partnerships with other organizations and institutions to support the mission.
